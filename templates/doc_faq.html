Pic Doctor Frequently Asked Questions

Who can become a Pic Doctor?

Due to restrictions from our payments provider, you must be a US resident with a bank account to become a Pic Doctor. We hope that this changes in the coming months!

How does it work?

We've got a full page to answer that question here.

How much am I paid?

Between 50% and 70% of the total. Your possible payout is calculated when you take a job. Two factors impact your cut:

  • Number of pictures accepted by users in the last 30 days
  • Your average rating

Why am I paid so little?

The remaining money covers lots of other expenses:

  • Credit card processing fees (up to 18% of a total transaction)
  • Bank transfer fees for the money that you earn
  • Advertising costs to supply easy work
  • Hosting costs
  • Employee costs

We hope that you'll find PicDoctors to be easy enough to use to be worth it.

How am I protected as a Doctor?

As a doctor we protect you by watermarking your images until a user has accepted your work as valid and complete. Likewise we've put a hold on the user's account to verify that they can pay for the work.

What is a merchant account, and why do I need it?

For an in depth look at merchant accounts view the wikipedia page here. Essentially a merchant account allows you to accept payment from a user. This is how you as a doctor will receive your payments. It's fully integrated into our site so you don't have to navigate between our site and our payment processing partner.

Who can see my work?

Right now, the job can only be seen if the user allows sharing.

What happens if the user isn't happy with my work?

We encourage users to request modifications to your work, but they have the option of returning the job to the market. If this happens, you won't be paid. We hope it doesn't happen often!

Can I communicate with the user?

Yep. Just use the 'comments' below the pictures, and we'll notify them.
